Baggage Allowance Guidelines

  1. 1
    Baggage Limit:
    • Each passenger is allowed to carry a certain amount of baggage free of charge. This limit is typically specified in terms of weight or number of bags.
  2. 2
    Standard Allowance:
    • Passengers are usually allowed to carry one or two pieces of luggage, with each piece not exceeding a certain weight limit, such as 20 kilograms per bag.
  3. 3
    Excess Baggage:
    • If a passenger exceeds the standard baggage allowance, they may be required to pay an excess baggage fee. This fee is calculated based on the weight or size of the additional baggage.
  4. 4
    Oversized Items:
    • Items such as bicycles, surfboards, or musical instruments that exceed standard baggage dimensions may require special handling and may incur additional charges.
  5. 5
    Restricted Items:
    • Certain items are not permitted on board due to safety or regulatory reasons. Passengers should check the list of prohibited items before packing their luggage.
  6. 6
    Valuables and Fragile Items:
    • Passengers are advised to carry valuables, fragile items, and essential medications in their carry-on luggage to ensure their safety and accessibility during the journey.
  7. 7
    Lost or Damaged Baggage:
    • GoBus! has a policy in place for handling lost or damaged baggage, including procedures for reporting incidents and filing claims for compensation.
  8. 8
    Special Requirements:
    • Passengers with special needs, such as medical equipment or mobility aids, should inform the bus company in advance to ensure appropriate arrangements can be made.
  9. 9
    Liability:
    • The bus company may have limitations on liability for lost, damaged, or delayed baggage. Passengers should review the company's terms and conditions regarding baggage liability.
  10. 10
    Additional Services:
    • Some bus companies may offer optional services such as baggage insurance or luggage delivery to provide passengers with added convenience and peace of mind.
